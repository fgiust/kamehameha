～時

# We use the word 時 to describe when something happens or happened.
# sentence A 時 + sentence B = When A, B.
# If, at the time the main event B takes place, A is current or is still "in the future," use the present tense in A.
# If, at the time of main event B, A is already "in the past," use the past tense in A.

When I go to America, I will get a visa.
Quando vado in America, prendo il visto.
アメリカに行[い]く時[とき]、ビザを取[と]ります

When I have gotten tired, I take a bath.
Quando sono stanco, faccio il bagno.
疲[つか]れた時[とき]、お風呂[ふろ]に入[はい]ります

When it rains, I take an umbrella.
Quando piove, porto un ombrello.
雨[あめ]が降[ふ]る時[とき]、傘[かさ]を持[も]って行[い]きます

When I was a child, I often went to the park.
Da bambino andavo spesso al parco.
子供[こども]の時[とき]、よく公園[こうえん]に行[い]きました

When I leave the house, I lock the door.
Quando esco di casa, chiudo la porta a chiave.
家[いえ]を出[で]る時[とき]、ドアに鍵[かぎ]をかけます

When I woke up this morning, I checked my phone.
Quando mi sono svegliato stamattina, ho controllato il telefono.
今朝[けさ]起[お]きた時[とき]、携帯[けいたい]を見[み]ました

When Ken has free time, he reads books.
Quando Ken ha tempo libero, legge libri.
けんさんが暇[ひま]な時[とき]、本[ほん]を読[よ]みます

When I lived in Osaka, I often ate takoyaki.
Quando vivevo a Osaka, mangiavo spesso takoyaki.
大阪[おおさか]に住[す]んでいた時[とき]、たこ焼[や]きをよく食[た]べました

When Michiko goes to the beach, she swims.
Quando Michiko va in spiaggia, nuota.
みちこさんが海[うみ]に行[い]く時[とき]、泳[およ]ぎます

When I did not understand, I asked the teacher.
Quando non capivo, chiedevo al professore.
分[わ]からなかった時[とき]、先生[せんせい]に聞[き]きました
